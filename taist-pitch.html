Every venue has its own zero-tables. Priced, that's ~€3k a year — a fraction of what they pay OpenTable for all their covers. This is the floor.

The ceiling — a second, independent check
Restaurants already spend 3–6% of revenue on marketing (Toast · ChowNow) — mostly on reach, regardless of fit. Applied to US full-service revenue alone ($533B, NRA), that's ~$16–32B/yr already being spent, badly.
Two separate methodologies, not additive: the floor is per-venue booking recapture; the ceiling is existing marketing spend redirected to performance. Comparable: OpenTable subscriptions run $3.6k–12k+/yr per venue before cover fees — and it seats 1.9B diners across 65k+ venues. See all sources →
Q&A · 02 "Why won't OpenTable or Google just build this?"
What they own

Inventory and the transaction. But being the booking pipe is exactly what gets you replaced — the model maker ships it natively, or anoints an exclusive partner. It already happened.

What we own

The layer beneath: an accumulating taste-and-context graph — who this diner is, what they chose, what they loved after. It compounds per person, and a foundation model can't retrain it into existence because it never sees it.

Positioning insight
Instead of buying a top slot, venues buy the data to optimize their positioning: which segments book them, where they win and lose, how to capture more of the intent already searching. Anonymized — never names.
The line to remember: we don't sell the top slot — we sell the insight to deserve it. That's the incumbent flaw we beat; pay-to-play would poison the match quality that is our moat.

Q&A · 06 "Why start in Israel — isn't it small?"

Contained, home turf, and owned by Ontopo, not OpenTable — so it's a partnership, not a war. We prove discovery and answer-quality here, and turn the proof into leverage.

Europe — the wedge

Fragmented rails, US giants barely present, incumbents in rare disarray — Quandoo closing, TheFork for sale, SevenRooms sold, OpenTable backlash. A timed window into an open door.

Q&A · 08 "How does Taist get invoked inside an assistant?"

A tool gets called three ways: the user connects it, the user names it, or the platform blesses it. Assistants increasingly won't auto-pick a pipe you didn't ask for — neutrality by design.

So we don't fight to be the silent default. We earn the diner's name recall through taste and memory — you return because it knows you — and because we're rail-agnostic, we're a partner to the platforms, not a threat they route around.

Q&A · 09 "What's the endgame?"
Independent scale

The taste graph compounds into a diner-facing discovery layer no one can retrain — a standalone company on an unownable asset.

Build-to-acquisition

The destabilized incumbents need exactly this layer and can't build it. The same asset makes us the obvious acquisition.

We design toward both — the unownable asset is what keeps either path open.
