02UNDERSTAND

Not just where — but why.

A flow's cost is rarely uniform. Variance, outliers, retry storms, runaway agentic loops, fallback-to-premium-model paths — Flux surfaces them automatically, so cost stops being a one-line number and starts being a story you can act on.

This is the part most tools skip. Billing dashboards average out the truth. Trace tools show you individual calls. Neither tells you that 1.4% of your support-summary calls are quietly consuming 47% of your support-summary budget — because token-heavy tickets silently fall back to a more expensive model.

What we don't promise: to be a debugger or APM. We're an economist for your AI, not your stack trace.
03JUSTIFY

Cost vs. intent. Not cost in a vacuum.

Cost isn't bad. Unjustified cost is bad. Flux tags every flow with its intent and projected envelope at build time — so spend gets compared to what you meant to build, not just to last month.

A $50K/mo flow that drives the core ROI metric is strategic. A $5K/mo flow tagged as "cost-sensitive deflection" that's silently using a premium model is the actual problem. The SDK captures intent, budget, and outcome contract — so every flow can be defended in front of the board, or surfaced as the answer to "what should we kill?"

What we don't promise: to define value for you. You set the intent. We measure spend against it.

04ACT

Recommendations defended, not just cheaper.

Every recommendation arrives with the evidence that justified it — the sample size, the quality delta, the intent it's serving, the projected savings. Open a pull request in one click, or dismiss with a reason that trains the model for next time.

"Switch to Haiku" is cheap advice — anyone can say it. "Switch to Haiku because we ran 1,200 of your production calls through both, the quality delta on your rubric was 6%, the intent was cost-sensitive deflection, and the savings are $8.2K/mo" is a board-ready recommendation. The difference is what the team will actually trust.

What we don't promise: to act for you. Recommendations are surfaced; you decide. Auto-action is opt-in, per flow.

Before you ask

Four questions every VP Eng asks.
Answered straight.

Does Flux see our prompts or completions?
No. The SDK is content-blind by default. Only metadata leaves your process — model, tokens, latency, flow tag, intent, cost. Prompt and completion content never touches our infrastructure. Customer data never trains our models.
What does the SDK add to my hot path?
Under 1ms. The wrapper is an SDK-level intercept, not a proxy. Telemetry emits asynchronously after the response returns. No extra network hop, no uptime dependency on our service. If Flux goes down, your AI calls don't.
How long does Tier 1 actually take?
5–10 minutes for your first provider. OAuth or a read-only API key. You'll see live spend, by model and team, before the meeting ends. No engineering ticket required.
What's the uninstall path?
Remove the wrapper. Done. Your existing AI code runs unchanged — we wrap your client, we don't replace it. No lock-in, no data migration, no goodbye. SOC 2 Type II in progress; VPC deployment available for enterprise.
