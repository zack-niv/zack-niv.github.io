# Taist — the pitch, told as a story

A spoken script for the click-through deck (`taist-pitch.html`).
One thought per slide. The slide holds the room; **your voice carries the message.**
Advance on the line marked **▸**. Press `S` in the deck to see these notes on screen while you rehearse.

Total runtime ≈ 3 minutes. Slow down. The pauses are the point.

---

### The weave
One person runs through the whole story: a diner planning a first date, asking for *a natural-wine bar with a terrace.* We open on their question. We close on Taist having answered it — and remembered it. Everything between is why that matters.

---

## ▸ Slide 1 — Taist *(cover)*
*(Let the name sit a beat. Don't rush. Then begin quietly, like you're telling a friend a story.)*

> "I want to tell you about a Thursday night."

## ▸ Slide 2 — The question
> "Someone is planning a first date. They don't open Google. They don't scroll a map. They just ask — out loud, almost — *'find me a natural-wine bar with a terrace, somewhere good for a first date.'*"

## ▸ Slide 3 — The answer
> "And the assistant answers. Not with ten blue links. Not with a page of options. With **three names**, in a single sentence. That is the whole world now. There is no second page."

## ▸ Slide 4 — The stakes *(dark)*
> "Now — if you run a restaurant, sit with that. For twenty years, being findable meant page one of Google, a pin on the map. That's gone. One answer, room for three. You are in that sentence… **or you do not exist.**"

## ▸ Slide 5 — The race
> "So everyone is racing to get into that sentence. There are two obvious ways. One — feed the AI better content, so it picks you. Two — become the tool it books through. Be the pipe."

## ▸ Slide 6 — The trap
> "Here's the trap. The content game? Anyone copies it by Tuesday — a race to the bottom. And the pipe? The giants already own it. OpenTable is the exclusive partner inside ChatGPT. Google owns the default. Be a popular pipe, and the platform either builds you natively — or hands your slot to someone else. **The pipe gets you replaced.**"

## ▸ Slide 7 — The insight
> "But look underneath. Below the content, below the booking, there's a third layer — and almost no one is standing on it. The giants own what's *available*. They own how you *pay*. But they do not own what you **love**. They don't own taste."

## ▸ Slide 8 — The moat *(dark)*
> "And taste — with memory — is the one thing a model cannot retrain into existence. Because it never sees it. It isn't on the public internet. It lives in the quiet accumulation of one person's choices, and what they loved afterward. That **compounds**. For you. And no one can take it."

## ▸ Slide 9 — Taist *(the name lands)*
> "That's **Taist**. The taste and context layer that sits inside the assistant. It knows the diner. It covers the long tail — the natural-wine bars and walk-in cafés the giants never even see. And it hands off, cleanly, to whoever can actually book the table. The connection is how we deliver. The taste is what we own."

## ▸ Slide 10 — Why now / where
> "And the moment is now. Discovery is being rewritten this year — and in Europe, the old guard is in disarray: one platform shutting down, one up for sale, one swallowed by a delivery company. So we prove it where we live — Tel Aviv, a market the giants haven't taken — and then we walk through the open door into Europe."

## ▸ Slide 11 — The close *(dark)*
> "And that first date — the wine bar, the terrace? **Taist** is what put it in the sentence. **Taist** is what booked it. And **Taist** is what remembers it — so the next time, the answer is even better."
>
> *(beat)*
>
> "When the world asks an AI where to eat… **Taist is the answer.**"

---

## Delivery notes
- **The numbers stay in your mouth, not on the wall.** "Three names," "twenty years," the three failing incumbents — say them, don't slide them.
- **Three dark slides (4, 8, 11) are your turns.** Drop your voice. Slow down. Let them land.
- **The name is withheld until slide 9.** Slides 1 and 9 both show "Taist," but on 9 it arrives as the *answer* the story earned. Lean into that.
- **The first date is the thread.** You open on their question and close on it being kept. If you only nail one thing, nail the return.
- **Keep visuals quiet on purpose.** If the room is reading the slide, they're not listening to you. The slide is a held breath; you are the sentence.
