Detection is solved. The value leaks in the human review window.

  • Reviews cluster late in the cycle — for hourly workforces, timesheets keep arriving until close to payday. That timing is real, not laziness.
  • But the screen is a flat table: nothing pulls controllers in, sequences the work, or shows progress.
  • "Review completed" is one self-declared checkbox — tickable having looked at nothing.

We can't move payday. We can make the window count — and start it as early as the data allows.

✓ The insight
04 / 12

A payroll cycle is a ceremony. Let's design it like one.

Every ceremony has three things. Each fixes a failure from slide 01 — and becomes a moment in the experience.

A date

Cadence & trigger

It's always before/after something. → A scheduled kickoff with a hard close.

Fixes: nothing pulls you to start.

A structure

Order of events

A defined sequence. → A guided worklist, biggest dollars first, with progress.

Fixes: flat table, no sequence.

Full attention

It matters

Everyone shows up. → Focus mode, next-best-actions, and shared accountability.

Fixes: shallow, chore-like effort.

✓ Execution · what it takes to ship
11 / 12

Config & assumptions.

What the ceremony needs to know
01Cycle dates — pulled from the payroll integration.
02Invite lead time — fire as data lands, before cutoff.
03Trigger event — "data ready + tests complete" opens the cycle.
04Routing rules — role → owner, per facility / financial area.
05Reason-code library — per alert type, seeded from history.
06Notify channels — email / Teams / Google Chat + SLA.
Next-best-action is driven by three sources: industry best practices · this account's historical resolutions of the same alert · the org's own policies & rules.
Working assumptions
Roles exist in the system → we can route, suggest an owner, or auto-route.
History & best-practice exist per recurring alert → powers next-best-action and reason codes.
Comments are nested & ready-on-click; a summary of the thread is emailed to mentioned owners (or via Teams / Google Chat).
Measure: A/B by facility · primary metric RSR · guardrail reversal rate.
